### Architectural Reasoning
- **Component Identification**: The source text describes a framework (RE4HCAI) for eliciting and modeling requirements in human-centered AI systems, with a case study on an AI-based system for enhancing 360° videos for VR. Core functional capabilities include enhancing video quality (e.g., resolution improvement by 4x, noise/artifacts removal) as a primary task of the AI system (Section 4.3-4.4). Quality constraints are tied to human-centered aspects across six areas, such as user needs (e.g., automation, invisibility), model needs (e.g., accuracy optimization, overfitting balance), and data needs (e.g., diversity, accuracy, completeness) (Sections 2-3, Tables 2-4).
- **Constraint Identification**: Non-functional aspects emphasize performance metrics (e.g., precision/recall trade-offs, loss functions), data quality (e.g., accuracy, completeness, consistency), and human factors (e.g., bias mitigation, perceptual quality via user feedback) to ensure unbiased, inclusive AI (Sections 2.1-2.3, 5.1). These are measurable via metrics like PSNR/SSIM and user studies (Table 3).
- **Functional Logic (FR-01)**: The primary role is the 'System' (AI/DL component) performing video enhancement tasks reactively and automatically for VR users, directly from case study requirements (Table 2, Fig. 8).
- **Quality Logic (NFR-01)**: The 'System' must satisfy constraints on data quality and model performance to support the functional enhancement, ensuring correctness and diversity (Tables 3-4, Section 4.6). This aligns with human-centered goals like QoE/QoS improvement without biases.
- **Quality Logic (NFR-02)**: Feedback and explainability constraints ensure trust and control, reporting metrics like perceptual rankings to calibrate user expectations (Sections 2.4-2.5, Table 5).
- **Domain Mapping**: For video enhancement (FR-01), applicable in VR entertainment and education (Section 4.4). For data quality (NFR-01), parallels in medical imaging or autonomous driving datasets. For trust/explainability (NFR-02), relevant to recommendation systems or healthcare AI.

Requirement ID: NFR-01  
Title: Data Quality and Diversity  
Description: The system must ensure data used for training meets quality standards including accuracy (e.g., filtering scene/motion changes), completeness (diverse attributes for motion, content, light), consistency (uniform resolution/length), and currentness (recent, continuous shots), with diversity thresholds to mitigate biases like under-representation of scenes/objects, while complying with privacy laws and responsible sourcing.
