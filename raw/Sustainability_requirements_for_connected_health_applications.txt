The aim of this paper is to define a reusable requirements repository (catalog) for sustainable connected health applications based on the main related software engineering standards, e-health tech- nology standards, and literature. The catalog contains requirements which cover the individual, social, environmental, and technical dimensions of sustainability. The requirements presented in this paper can be very useful to connected health application stakeholders and can be adapted to specific m-health applications. Moreover, the requirements catalog can be used to generate a checklist of requirements to evaluate and audit connected health applications. An application example is presented to show that the evaluation of connected health applications using the generated checklist is realistic.  KEYWORDS  apps, catalog, connected health, requirements, sustainability  1   INTRODUCTION  There is a need to promote patient-self management, patient education from a very early stage, healthy lifestyle, and independence of patients. 1   All of these can be promoted through connected health (CH) applications. Connected health can be defined as “the collective term for telecare, tele- health, telemedicine, mHealth, digital health and eHealth services.”   2   The CH technologies are transforming health care   3   and empowering patients as they are assuming greater responsibility for their own health care decisions.   4   , 5   In fact, CH can improve health care outcomes by connecting patients with caregivers and clinicians.   6   They empower people, 7   , 8   provide an emotional support,   9   raise awareness about chronic diseases, 10   and improve the access to supportive care. 11   The most trending CH applications are m-health applications, also known as m-health apps. M-health apps, such as apps to monitor a particular condition 12   , 13   and mobile personal health records (mPHRs), 14   , 15   which allow patients to access and coordinate their health information through their mobile devices, will play a more dominant role in CH future.   16   Many apps have been used in a variety of health areas to improve patients' lives and health.   17   - 19  Sustainability.   T o promote a large-scale adoption of CH apps, there is a need to promote their sustainability. Achieving sustainability of health care systems is one of the objectives of research and innovation financed by the EU.   20   The definition which is the most widely used in literature   21 of sustainable development has been given by the United Nations (UN) as the ability to “meet the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to satisfy their own needs.” 22   According to the UN, sustainability needs to satisfy 3 dimensions: (1) economic, (2) social, and (3) environmental. 22   In addition to these dimensions, 2 other dimensions have been associated to software sustainability 21   , 23   : (4) individual (or human) dimension and (5) technical dimension. Software sustainability is therefore concerned with the economic, individual, social, environment, and technical dimensions which may be positively impacted by CH apps. 24   Several studies   25   - 27   have pointed out that requirements engineering is playing a vital role in software sustainability. Therefore, there is a need for a requirements catalog which provides different stakeholders of CH apps with different requirements set to develop and/or audit sustainable CH apps. In this paper, sustainability is defined as the extent to which an app is adopted and maintained to achieve durability and continuity in use in a given context. 28   T o the best of our knowledge, no standard on sustainability has been proposed up to now. The main objective of this paper is to develop a reusable catalog for sustainable CH apps. As far as we know, there is no complete and well-defined set of requirements for sustainable CH app environments. This paper is a step forward in this direction. The inherent properties determine the capabilities of a software product.   30   They are permanent features and are opposed to assigned properties such as the software product price. The inherent properties are part of software product requirements which can be classi- fied as either functional requirements or software quality requirements. Functional requirements determine what the software product is able to do (eg, formatting some text or modulating a signal) while quality requirements determine how well the software product performs (eg, usability requirements or performance efficiency requirements).   30   Software quality requirements help to develop sustainable software, 23   thus sustainable CH apps. These requirements are transversal to any kind of apps (PHR, prevention, treatment, and monitoring). In contrast, functional requirements are particular to a kind of apps (PHR, prevention, treatment, and monitoring). The SCH-CAT is based on the standards and recommendations from literature and is generated using requirements engineering techniques.   31  Requirements engineering. In this paper, we are concerned more about   green by software   requirements. Cabot et al   41   have observed that there is a lack of standards definitions for sustainability concepts. In their study, they argue that requirements engineers should work on defining taxonomies for sustainability concepts. Many researchers 27   , 42   have tried to identify sustainable requirements or green requirements; however, they have focused only on requirements regarding software energy consumption. Sustainability should be defined according to the 5 aforementioned dimensions.   21 Penzenstadler 23   explains that we should achieve a balance among the 5 aforementioned dimensions of sustainability to achieve the overall sustainability of our daily lives. Each dimension is concerned by a set of requirements.   23  •   Individual sustainability refers to the maintenance of the individual human capital, eg, health, education, skills, and access to services. 43 Individual sustainability can be covered by privacy, safety, security, human-computer interaction, usability, personal health, and well-being.   23  •   Social sustainability aims to preserve the social capital and preserve services and solidarity of social communities. 43   Social sustainability can be handled via computer-supported collaborative work   23   that aims to strengthening community building and improve community interaction.  •   Economic sustainability aims to maintain capital assets and added-value (interest) assets. This paper presents a reusable requirement catalog for sustainable CH apps without a limitation to a specific context.  3   SCH-CAT: A REUSABLE REQUIREMENTS CATALOG FOR SUSTAINABLE CH APPS  This section presents the development process of the SCH-CAT and the list of sources analyzed to select requirements for sustainable CH apps.  3.1   Requirements specification  The content of a CH apps is very critical to its adoption by users therefore to its sustainability. Sustainable CH apps should be useful to their users; for this reason, a special attention should be taken while choosing information to be displayed to users. In addition to the content of the apps, there are other factors that influence the acceptability and adoption of CH apps, mainly software quality and i18n requirements. Therefore, requirements of i18n and software quality requirements, especially requirements related to the operability of the app, should be considered in the development/evaluation of sustainability. Requirements for the SCH-CAT have been identified from literature reviews on CH apps   50   - 55   and from previous research dealing with software quality, 56   - 58   i18n, 48   , 59   , 60   and sustainability. 21   , 23   , 24   , 49   Moreover, requirements from the following standards were also extracted:  •   ISO/IEC 25010 standard 29   for system and software product quality requirements and evaluation  •   ISO 9241-151 standard   61   for ergonomics of human-system interaction  •   ISO/TR 18307 standard   62   for interoperability and compatibility in trusted health information interchange between software apps and systems in health care  •   ISO/HL7 27931 standard   63   for data exchange in health care environments  •   ISO 21090 standard   64   for information interchange in health informatics  •   ISO/TR 14292 standard   65   for the definition, scope, and context of personal health records  •   ISO/TR 20514 standard   66   for the definition, scope, and context of electronic health records  •   ISO/TS 14265 standard   67   for processing personal health information  •   ISO 27799 standard   68   for information security management in health  •   The W3C standards   69   for Web and mobile devices Requirements based on Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 recommendations   70   , 71   have been included in the catalog. Furthermore, usability guidelines from app repositories   72   have been taken into consideration in addition to requirements from software engineering for sustainability project. 73   After identifying the standards and other information sources, the corresponding parts of text relevant to sustainability of CH apps have been identified, analyzed, and specified.  3.2   Catalog development  The quality of the SRS documentation, which serves as an input to the design, coding, and testing phases, is critical to the success of any software project. 74   Therefore, for the catalog development, guidelines from the IEEE 29148 standard   38   have been followed. Traceability relationships are used to define relationships between the requirements identified which facilitates both reuse and maintenance tasks. As much as possible, we have followed the principles of minimal coupling and maximal cohesion to reduce the degree of interdependency (low coupling) and increase the existing conceptual and functional relationships (high cohesion) among requirements. Sustainability requirements should be considered at early stages in the software development phase. However, software engineers treat sustainability as a postscript as they are driven by time-to-market pressure and do not have time to apply sustainability guidelines.   79   For this reason, the SCH-CAT can be very beneficial to facilitate the work of CH developers which will lead to the development of more sustainable CH apps. The SCH-CAT can also be very beneficial for different CH app stakeholders. In fact, developers can use all or some requirements of this catalog to develop sustainable CH apps. Health organizations or development companies which are willing to promote CH via apps can elicit, specify and validate their requirements from the SCH-CAT. Moreover, the sustainable requirements are adaptable and can be reused for specific CH apps such as apps for cardiology or oncology. Furthermore, audit organizations or CH app stakeholders can use the SCH-CAT to evaluate and/or audit CH apps. A checklist can be generated from the SCH-CAT. This checklist should include a set of requirements that will constitute the list of items to be checked and can be adapted to the CH app to be evaluated. T o that end, a reuse-based approach to generate new requirements documents from the SCH-CAT should be applied as shown in the application example. This step is necessary, since the variation points of the reusable requirements, which are basically parameterized requirements and traceability relationships, must be resolved in reuse time in accordance with the system evaluation and/or audit. The requirements documents will then be mapped onto items of the Checklist in order to carry out the evaluation and/or the audit of CH apps. This study may have several limitations, such as other sources relevant to the generation catalog may have not been taking into consideration. However, the fact that requirements were elicited from several sources in software engineering, e-health technology and literature about sustain- ability may have alleviated this issue. Also, using only one app in the application example may not be sufficient to demonstrate the applicability of the catalog. However, the application example was presented to give insights on how to use the SCH-CAT to generate a checklist that can be used in the evaluation of CH apps. The SCH-CAT has certain contextual bounding (e.g. suggesting social media as a login), the findings must be therefore interpreted in light of these contexts. Requirements about the economic dimension of sustainability have been omitted from the SCH-CAT, there- fore, the catalog does not cover all aspects of sustainability. However, such requirements are linked to the assigned properties of the CH software product and they are subject to change without including changes in the CH app's source code.  6   CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK  In spite of the increasing interest in sustainability in our digital-driven globalized world, no specific standards dealing with sustainability were found.
