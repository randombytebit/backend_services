Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-01  
Title: Positive Individual Impact  
Description: The app shall have a positive individual impact by respecting user security and privacy (e.g., via authentication procedures allowing user choice on authentication and login/social network access) and promoting personal health and well-being (e.g., via health recommendations and personal health record management).  
Citation: Table 2 (Section 3.7.6.1), Page 5  
Related Title: mHealth Personal Health Records (mPHRs), Chronic Disease Self-Management Apps, Patient Wellness Trackers  

Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: FR-01  
Title: Personal Data Management  
Description: The app shall allow the user to manage personal information in the profile, including editing, removing, adding, and sharing personal data, while considering country-specific legislation on user ownership of personal information.  
Citation: Table 2 (1.1.2 and sub-requirements), Page 5  
Related Title: Blood Donation Apps, Mobile Personal Health Records, User Profile Management in Telemedicine Apps  

Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: FR-02  
Title: Personal Health Record Management  
Description: The app shall enable the user to manage their personal health record, including consulting health recommendations, editing, removing, adding, sharing, importing, exporting, and backing up health record data.  
Citation: Table 2 (1.2 and sub-requirements), Page 5  
Related Title: Electronic Health Record (EHR) Mobile Interfaces, Pregnancy Monitoring Apps, Cardiovascular Patient Trackers  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-02  
Title: Positive Social Impact  
Description: The app shall have a positive social impact by promoting interaction among users via social networks and/or online healthcare forums, promoting social solidarity via a rewards system, and being designed for cultural diversity and multilingual use (e.g., adapting content, help, terminology, text alignment, formats, units, and advertisements to user language preferences).  
Citation: Table 3 (Section 3.7.6.2), Page 6  
Related Title: Community Health Forums, Social Solidarity Apps like Blood Donation Rewards, Culturally Adaptive mHealth Apps  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-03  
Title: Positive Environmental Impact  
Description: The app shall have a positive environmental impact by reducing transportation needs (e.g., finding nearby healthcare centers and enabling data interchange with EHRs/PHRs/third parties), being convenient for frequent use (e.g., using pictures, icons, and graphs), and connecting to other IT resources (e.g., data backups, social networks, Bluetooth/NFC, maps repositories).  
Citation: Table 4 (Section 3.7.6.3), Page 6  
Related Title: Location-Based Telehealth Apps, Eco-Friendly Health Monitoring Systems, Integrated Clinical Environment (ICE) Apps  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-04  
Title: Positive Technical Impact  
Description: The app shall have a positive technical impact by easily adapting to future OS changes (e.g., respecting mobile OS guidelines and platform adaptation), being energy-efficient (e.g., shutting down in idle mode and supporting different profiles on the same device), and enabling offline access (e.g., access, editing, and saving data without Internet connection).  
Citation: Table 5 (Section 3.7.6.4), Page 6  
Related Title: Longevity-Focused mHealth Apps, Energy-Efficient Wearable Health Trackers, Offline-Capable Telecare Systems  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-05  
Title: Sustainability Requirements Structure  
Description: The app's requirements specification shall follow IEEE 29148 structure, including a dedicated sustainability subsection (3.7.6) covering individual, social, environmental, and technical impacts (excluding economic as non-inherent), with attributes like ID, source, priority, and traceability for reuse, minimal coupling, and maximal cohesion.  
Citation: Table 1 and Section 3.3, Pages 4-5  
Related Title: Reusable Requirements Catalogs for eHealth, Audit Checklists for CH Apps, Software Product Requirements Specifications (SRS) for Sustainable Systems
