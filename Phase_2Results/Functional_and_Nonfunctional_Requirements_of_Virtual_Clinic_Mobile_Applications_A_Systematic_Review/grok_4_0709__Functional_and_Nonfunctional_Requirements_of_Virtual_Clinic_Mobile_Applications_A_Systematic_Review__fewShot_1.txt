Requirement ID: FR-01  
Title: Demographic Data Documentation  
Description: The system should capture and store user demographic information, including identity details (first name and surname) and insurance information (insurance name and number), to facilitate personalized virtual clinic interactions and administrative processes.

Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: FR-02  
Title: Health Record Management  
Description: The system should enable documentation and management of comprehensive health records, including vital signs (e.g., blood pressure, heart rate), kidney-related data (e.g., blood sugar), fitness metrics (e.g., weight, exercise, sleep, nutrition), skin conditions, history (e.g., disease history, family history, allergies, medications, vaccinations, surgeries, smoking), present history (e.g., symptoms, X-rays, tests, treatments, prescriptions, patient training), inpatient treatment history (e.g., admission/discharge dates, hospital name, chief complaint, summary), and medication details (e.g., name, dose, type, instructions, side effects). This ensures accurate tracking and sharing of patient health data for virtual consultations.  
Citation: Table 1 (Page 4), under "B. Health record" and its subcategories (Vital sign documentation, Kidney documentation, Fitness documentation, Skin documentation, History documentation, Present history documentation, Documentation of inpatient treatment history, Medication documentation).  
Related Title: Electronic Health Record (EHR) Mobile Integrations, Chronic Disease Monitoring Apps, Remote Patient Monitoring Systems for Diabetes or Cardiovascular Care  

Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: FR-03  
Title: General User Interface Features  
Description: The system should provide general UI features including reminders (e.g., for appointments and preventive care), display capabilities (e.g., patient's self-declaration of health status, consultant identity with name, surname, profile picture, curriculum vitae including expertise and consulting field, type of communication such as phone call, text, video call, real-time, and appointment details like date, time, duration, waiting time), service selection (e.g., choosing consultation day, time, and insurance type), reporting (e.g., weekly/monthly reports on treatment process, vital signs, and medical history), support (e.g., documentation of communication ways like tagging in VCMA, frequently asked questions, Q&A forums with patients and consultants), and help/tutorial (e.g., text guides, in-app training, "About us", terms and conditions). These features enhance user engagement and facilitate seamless virtual clinic operations.  
Citation: Table 1 (Page 4), under "C. General features of UI" and its subcategories (Reminders, Display, Service selection, Reporting, Support, Help/tutorial).  
Related Title: Virtual Consultation Scheduling Apps, Patient Engagement Platforms in Telehealth, Mobile Apps for Appointment Management in Outpatient Care
