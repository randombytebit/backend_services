### Architectural Reasoning for Virtual Clinic Mobile Application (VCMA) Requirements

- **Source Text Analysis**: The provided document, "Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review" by Zahra Parsaei et al., offers a detailed examination of the requirements for VCMAs. It categorizes requirements into functional (demographic data documentation, health record management, and user interface features) and nonfunctional (usability, accessibility, compatibility, efficiency, and security) themes, extracted through a systematic review of 17 studies.
- **Component Identification**: The functional requirements focus on core capabilities of the VCMA, such as documenting patient data, managing health records, and providing a user-friendly interface for interactions like scheduling and communication. Nonfunctional requirements address quality constraints that ensure the system’s effectiveness, such as usability for diverse users, security of sensitive data, and compatibility across platforms.
- **Constraint Identification**: The text specifies constraints like the need for secure data exchange (security), ease of use for varying literacy levels (usability), and the ability to operate across different devices and systems (compatibility). Efficiency in transactions and accessibility features for saving and sharing data are also highlighted as critical.
- **Functional Logic**: The primary role for functional requirements is the 'System' as it performs tasks like data documentation, health record management, and user interaction through the UI. These are essential for enabling virtual clinical services for patients and healthcare providers.
- **Quality Logic**: For nonfunctional requirements, the 'System' must adhere to quality standards such as usability to ensure user engagement, security to protect patient data, and efficiency to support seamless operations. These constraints are necessary to maintain trust and functionality in a virtual healthcare environment.
- **Domain Mapping**: Functional requirements are applicable in domains like telemedicine platforms for chronic disease management and remote consultation systems. Nonfunctional requirements align with domains such as secure health data exchange systems, cross-platform healthcare apps, and user-friendly medical interfaces for diverse populations.
