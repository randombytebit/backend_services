Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: FR-01  
Title: Demographic Data Documentation  
Description: The system must enable documentation of patient demographic data, including identity details such as first name and surname, and insurance information such as insurance name and number, to support user registration and service eligibility verification in virtual clinic interactions.  
Citation: Table 1 (Page 4), Parsaei et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications.

Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: FR-02  
Title: Health Record Management  
Description: The system must facilitate comprehensive health record management, including documentation of vital signs (e.g., blood pressure, heart rate), kidney-related metrics (e.g., blood sugar), fitness data (e.g., weight, exercise, sleep, nutrition), skin condition, medical history (e.g., disease history, family history, allergies, medications, vaccinations, surgeries, smoking), present history (e.g., symptoms, conditions, X-rays, tests, treatments, prescriptions, patient training), inpatient treatment history (e.g., admission/discharge dates, hospital name, chief complaint, summary), and medication details (e.g., name, dose, type, instructions, side effects), to enable monitoring, clinical decision-making, and patient self-management.  
Citation: Table 1 (Page 4), Section 3.3 (Page 3), Parsaei et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications.

Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: FR-03  
Title: General User Interface Features  
Description: The system must provide general user interface features, including reminders for appointments and preventive care; displays for patient self-declarations on health status, consultant identity (name, surname, profile picture), curriculum vitae (expertise, consulting field), communication types (phone call, text, video call, real-time), and appointment details (date, time, duration, waiting time); service selection for choosing consultation day, time, and insurance type; reporting of weekly/monthly treatment processes, vital signs, and medical history; support via documentation of communication methods (tagging, FAQs) and Q&A forums for discussions with patients and consultants; and help/tutorials including text guides, in-app training, about us section, and terms/conditions of use, to enhance user interaction and accessibility in virtual consultations.  
Citation: Table 1 (Page 4), Section 3.3 (Page 3), Section 5 (Page 6), Parsaei et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications.

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-01  
Title: Usability  
Description: The system must ensure usability through settings features such as creating personal profiles for users (consultants and patients), enabling microphone and camera access, allowing selection of measurement units (e.g., centimeters and meters for height), supporting video calls with other participants, providing options for selecting conversation starters, and including a log-out function, to make the application intuitive and user-friendly for virtual clinic operations.
