**Functional Requirement 1**  
Role: User  
Requirement ID: FR-01  
Title: Demographic Data Documentation  
Description: The system must allow users to document demographic data including identity details (first name and surname) and insurance information (name and number) to ensure accurate patient identification and billing processes. Validation should include automated checks for data completeness and format correctness.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Volume 2024, Article ID 7800321, Table 1.  
Related Title: Telemedicine Patient Registration Systems, Chronic Disease Management Apps  

**Functional Requirement 2**  
Role: User  
Requirement ID: FR-02  
Title: Health Record Management  
Description: The system must enable users to manage comprehensive health records including vital signs (blood pressure, heart rate), kidney documentation (blood sugar), fitness data (weight, exercise, sleep, nutrition), skin conditions, medical history (disease, family history, allergies, medications, vaccinations, surgeries, smoking), present history (symptoms, X-rays, tests, treatments, prescriptions), inpatient treatment history, and medication details (name, dose, type, instructions, side effects). Validation should involve cross-verification with clinical standards and periodic user confirmation to ensure data accuracy for AI-driven decision support tools.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Volume 2024, Article ID 7800321, Table 1.  
Related Title: Electronic Health Record (EHR) Integration Systems, AI-Driven Clinical Decision Support Apps  

**Functional Requirement 3**  
Role: User  
Requirement ID: FR-03  
Title: General Features of User Interface (UI)  
Description: The system must provide UI features including reminders (appointments, preventive care), display options (patient health status, consultant identity and profile, communication type such as phone, text, video call), appointment scheduling (date, time, duration, waiting time), service selection (consultation day, time, insurance type), reporting (weekly/monthly treatment and vital sign reports), support (communication tagging, FAQs, discussion forums), and help/tutorials (text guides, in-app training, terms of use). Validation should include user acceptance testing (UAT) to ensure intuitiveness and functionality for diverse user groups.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Volume 2024, Article ID 7800321, Table 1.  
Related Title: Patient Engagement Platforms, Virtual Consultation Scheduling Systems  

**Non-Functional Requirement 1**  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-01  
Title: Usability  
Description: The system must ensure usability by supporting features such as personal profile creation, microphone and camera settings, measurement unit selection (e.g., centimeters, meters), video call capabilities with multiple participants, conversation starter selection, and logout functionality. Validation should involve usability testing with diverse user groups to achieve a minimum user satisfaction score (e.g., 80% positive feedback) and ensure intuitive interaction for AI-enhanced interfaces.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Volume 2024, Article ID 7800321, Table 2.  
Related Title: User-Centric Telehealth Interfaces, AI-Powered Virtual Care Platforms  

**Non-Functional Requirement 2**  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-02  
Title: Accessibility  
Description: The system must ensure accessibility by allowing users to save and mark appointments, reminders, notes, recorded voices, photos, and messages; send feedback on consultant performance; select text from default lists; and share individual lessons and health record information with doctors. Validation should include accessibility testing per WCAG 2.1 standards to ensure compliance for users with disabilities, with a confidence level of at least 95% in accessibility audit results.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., et al. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Volume 2024, Article ID 7800321, Table 2.  
Related Title: Inclusive Telemedicine Solutions, Accessible Health Data Sharing Systems

Role: System

**Non-Functional Requirement 5**  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-05  
Title: Security
