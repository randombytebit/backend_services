Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-01  
Title: Usability  
Description: System should support usability features including creation of personal profiles for users (consultants and patients), access to microphone and camera functionalities, selection of measurement units (e.g., centimeters and meters for height), video calls with connected participants, selection of conversation starters, and secure log out options to ensure intuitive and effective user interaction during virtual consultations.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., Jangi, M., Tahmasebian, S., & Ehteshami, A. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Article ID 7800321.  
Related Title: User-Friendly Telemedicine Interfaces, Intuitive Virtual Health Platforms  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-02  
Title: Accessibility  
Description: System should enable accessibility through services such as saving and marking appointments, reminders, notes, recorded voices, photos, and messages; allowing users to send feedback on consultant performance and select predefined text options; and providing the ability to share individual lessons, health records with doctors, and facilitate patient-consultant data exchange to ensure inclusive access for diverse users.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., Jangi, M., Tahmasebian, S., & Ehteshami, A. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Article ID 7800321.  
Related Title: Inclusive mHealth Accessibility Features, Barrier-Free Virtual Care Systems  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-03  
Title: Compatibility  
Description: System should ensure compatibility with various service formats, including recording and integration of insurance details (name and number), to support seamless operation across different devices, networks, and healthcare ecosystems without disrupting virtual clinic functionalities.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., Jangi, M., Tahmasebian, S., & Ehteshami, A. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Article ID 7800321.  
Related Title: Cross-Device Telehealth Compatibility, Insurance-Integrated Mobile Health Apps  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-04  
Title: Efficiency  
Description: System should promote efficiency by providing financial transaction reporting services, optimizing resource use, and minimizing delays in virtual interactions (e.g., through scheduled visits and real-time responses) to ensure reliable runtime performance and timely delivery of healthcare services.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., Jangi, M., Tahmasebian, S., & Ehteshami, A. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Article ID 7800321.  
Related Title: Performance-Optimized Virtual Clinics, Resource-Efficient Remote Monitoring Systems  

Non-Functional Requirement  
Role: System  
Requirement ID: NFR-05  
Title: Security  
Description: System should enforce security measures including multiple login methods (text password, email, activation code, or anonymous access), user identity verification via mobile verification codes, options to delete consultation records, documents, and bank transactions, and encrypted exchange of patient data to maintain runtime trustworthiness, privacy, and protection against unauthorized access in AI-assisted virtual care components.  
Citation: Parsaei, Z., Jangi, M., Tahmasebian, S., & Ehteshami, A. (2024). Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements of Virtual Clinic Mobile Applications: A Systematic Review. International Journal of Telemedicine and Applications, Article ID 7800321.  
Related Title: Secure Data Exchange in Telemedicine, Privacy-Focused AI Health Platforms
