### Architectural Reasoning

- **Component Identification**: The source text discusses the development of SofiHub, a smart home system for the elderly, emphasizing functionalities such as passive monitoring, reminders, anomaly detection, and emergency assistance. These are core functional capabilities aimed at supporting independent living.
- **Constraint Identification**: The text also highlights non-functional constraints, particularly around emotional goals such as reducing loneliness, ensuring safety, maintaining privacy, and ensuring system integration into users' lives. These are critical quality indicators for user acceptance and adoption.
- **Functional Logic**: The primary role is the 'System' (SofiHub) which performs tasks like issuing reminders, detecting anomalies, and supporting independent living through non-intrusive means (e.g., motion sensors).
- **Quality Logic**: The 'System' and 'User' must address emotional and usability constraints. Emotional goals like feeling safe, less lonely, and in control are non-functional requirements that directly impact the system's success and user satisfaction. Usability and privacy are also key non-functional aspects to ensure the system is non-intrusive and acceptable to users.
- **Domain Mapping**: For functional tasks like anomaly detection and reminders, domains such as elderly care facilities and personal health monitoring systems are relevant. For emotional goals like reducing loneliness and ensuring safety, parallel domains include social companion systems, mental health support platforms, and security systems for vulnerable populations.
