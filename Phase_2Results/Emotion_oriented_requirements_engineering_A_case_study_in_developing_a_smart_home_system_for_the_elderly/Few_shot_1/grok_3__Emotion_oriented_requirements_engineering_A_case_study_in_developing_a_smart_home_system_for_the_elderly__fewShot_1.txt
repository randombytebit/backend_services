Below are the extracted Functional (FR) and Non-Functional (NFR) requirements from the provided document titled "Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly." The requirements are formatted according to the specified rules and are derived from various sections of the paper, focusing on the SofiHub smart home system for elderly users.

### Functional Requirements (FR)

**Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: FR-01  
- **Title**: Support Independent Living  
- **Description**: The system must support independent living for elderly users by providing functionalities such as passive monitoring through non-intrusive sensors, issuing reminders for hydration and medication, and detecting anomalous behaviors (e.g., prolonged time in bed or irregular bathroom visits). The system should learn user routines and behaviors to adapt notifications and support daily activities without making users feel monitored or dependent.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.1 (SofiHub Features), Section 4.2 (SofiHub Emotion-Oriented Requirements Engineering)  
- **Related Title**: Elderly Assisted Living Systems, Smart Home Automation for Seniors, Independent Living Support Platforms  

**Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: FR-02  
- **Title**: Issue User-Specific Reminders and Notifications  
- **Description**: The system must issue user-specific reminders for appointments, special events, hydration, and medication, triggered by user activity or predefined schedules. Notifications should be customizable to avoid overwhelming users and should be delivered in a friendly, non-intrusive manner (e.g., morning messages).  
- **Citation**: Section 4.1 (SofiHub Features), Section 4.2 (SofiHub Emotion-Oriented Requirements Engineering)  
- **Related Title**: Personal Health Reminder Apps, Smart Home Notification Systems, Elderly Care Reminder Tools  

**Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: FR-03  
- **Title**: Detect and Alert on Anomalous Situations  
- **Description**: The system must detect anomalous situations such as falls, unusual inactivity (e.g., spending abnormal time in bed), or security risks (e.g., doors left open) using sensors. Upon detection, the system should issue alerts to carers, relatives, or service providers to ensure timely intervention.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.1 (SofiHub Features), Section 5.2.2 (Feeling Safe and Reassured)  
- **Related Title**: Fall Detection Systems, Smart Home Security Alerts, Elderly Emergency Response Platforms  

**Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: FR-04  
- **Title**: Carer Interface for Monitoring and Reports  
- **Description**: The system must provide a carer interface that allows family members or professional carers to monitor user activity remotely and receive customizable reports summarizing the elderly user’s behavior and well-being status.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.1 (SofiHub Features)  
- **Related Title**: Remote Care Monitoring Apps, Family Care Coordination Tools, Elderly Health Tracking Systems  

**Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: FR-05  
- **Title**: Automate Physical Environment  
- **Description**: The system must automate aspects of the physical environment, such as heating and cooling, based on user preferences or learned behavior to enhance comfort and convenience for elderly users.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.1 (SofiHub Features)  
- **Related Title**: Smart Home Climate Control Systems, IoT Environmental Automation Tools, Elderly Comfort Optimization Platforms

### Non-Functional Requirements (NFR)

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-01  
- **Title**: Privacy and Data Security  
- **Description**: The system must ensure the privacy of elderly users by securing all data collected through sensors. Data should be stored locally within the hub and accessible only to authorized personnel (e.g., as approved by ethics committees). The system must prevent unauthorized access or hacking of sensitive information to maintain user trust and alleviate concerns about being monitored.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.2 (SofiHub Emotion-Oriented Requirements Engineering), Section 5.2.5 (Privacy and Invasiveness)  
- **Related Title**: IoT Privacy Protection Systems, Smart Home Data Security Solutions, Elderly Monitoring Privacy Tools  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-02  
- **Title**: Emotional Goal: Feeling Safe  
- **Description**: The system must be designed to make elderly users feel safe by ensuring that anomalies (e.g., falls or security risks) are detected and escalated promptly to carers or service providers. This emotional goal should be prioritized to reassure both users and their relatives about the user’s well-being.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.2 (SofiHub Emotion-Oriented Requirements Engineering), Section 5.2.2 (Feeling Safe and Reassured)  
- **Related Title**: Elderly Safety Assurance Systems, Smart Home Emergency Response Platforms, Personal Security IoT Solutions  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-03  
- **Title**: Emotional Goal: Feeling Independent  
- **Description**: The system must be designed to support elderly users’ sense of independence by minimizing intrusive monitoring and avoiding features that make users feel dependent on others. Functionalities should empower users to live as they please without constant oversight, balancing support with autonomy.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.2 (SofiHub Emotion-Oriented Requirements Engineering), Section 5.2.1 (Reduced Loneliness)  
- **Related Title**: Independent Living Support Systems, Non-Intrusive Elderly Care Platforms, Autonomy-Focused Smart Homes  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-04  
- **Title**: Emotional Goal: Feeling In Control  
- **Description**: The system must ensure that elderly users feel in control of their lives and the technology by limiting the frequency and type of interactions (e.g., restricting messages to essential reminders like morning messages and medication alerts) to avoid overwhelming them or making them feel controlled by the system.  
- **Citation**: Section 4.2 (SofiHub Emotion-Oriented Requirements Engineering), Section 5.2.4 (In Control)  
- **Related Title**: User-Controlled Smart Home Systems, Elderly Autonomy IoT Tools, Personalized Interaction Platforms  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-05  
- **Title**: Emotional Goal: Feeling Less Lonely and Cared About  
- **Description**: The system must address the emotional needs of elderly users to feel less lonely and cared about by providing regular, friendly interactions (e.g., varied morning messages) that simulate companionship. This should be balanced with maintaining independence and control to avoid over-dependence on the system.  
- **Citation**: Section 5.2.1 (Reduced Loneliness), Section 5.3 (Implications of Findings on the Next Generation of SofiHub)  
- **Related Title**: Social Isolation Mitigation Systems, Elderly Companionship Apps, Emotional Support Smart Home Tools  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-06  
- **Title**: Emotional Goal: System Integration into Daily Life  
- **Description**: The system must be seamlessly integrated into the daily lives of elderly users to minimize negative perceptions of technology as complex or frustrating. It should feel like a natural part of their routine (e.g., through non-intrusive interactions and simple interfaces) to enhance adoption and sustained usage.  
- **Citation**: Section 5.2.3 (System is Integrated in User’s Life)  
- **Related Title**: User-Friendly Smart Home Systems, Elderly Technology Adoption Platforms, Routine-Integrated IoT Solutions  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-07  
- **Title**: Reliability and Minimal Disruption  
- **Description**: The system must operate reliably with minimal disruptions due to power failures or technical issues. It should ensure timely delivery of messages and alerts to avoid user frustration or anxiety about system performance (e.g., concerns about upsetting or confusing the system).  
- **Citation**: Section 5.2.7 (Emotional Threats)  
- **Related Title**: High-Reliability Smart Home Systems, Elderly Care Dependability Tools, IoT System Stability Platforms  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role**: System  
- **Requirement ID**: NFR-08  
- **Title**: Usability for Elderly Users  
- **Description**: The system must be designed with high usability for elderly users, ensuring simplicity and ease of interaction to reduce fear or frustration with technology. Features should be intuitive, with minimal learning curve, and interactions should feel comfortable and confidence-building.  
- **Citation**: Section 5.2.6 (Other Views on Using SofiHub)  
- **Related Title**: Elderly-Friendly IoT Interfaces, Usable Smart Home Solutions, Senior-Centric Technology Platforms
