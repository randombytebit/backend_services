Below is the extracted set of functional and non-functional requirements from the provided technical documentation on the SofiHub smart home system for the elderly. **Functional Requirement**  
**Role:** System  
**Requirement ID:** FR-01  
**Title:** Support Independent Living  
**Description:** The system must support independent living for elderly users by providing assistive services such as reminders for hydration and medication, identifying security risks (e.g., open doors), detecting anomalous situations (e.g., prolonged time in bed), and automating the physical environment (e.g., heating, cooling).  
**Citation:** Curumsing, M. K., et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. [Section 4.1]  
**Related Title:** Assisted Living Smart Home for Elderly Care

**Functional Requirement**  
**Role:** System  
**Requirement ID:** FR-02  
**Title:** Issue User-Specific Reminders  
**Description:** The system must issue user-specific reminders for appointments, special events, hydration, and medication, triggered by user activity to ensure timely and relevant notifications.  
**Citation:** Curumsing, M. K., et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. [Section 4.1]  
**Related Title:** Medication Adherence System for Chronic Illness Management

**Functional Requirement**  
**Role:** System  
**Requirement ID:** FR-03  
**Title:** Detect Anomalous Behavior  
**Description:** The system must detect anomalous behavior, such as a user spending an abnormal amount of time in bed, and issue alerts to carers or family members to ensure timely intervention.  
**Citation:** Curumsing, M. K., et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. [Section 4.1]  
**Related Title:** Fall Detection System in Elderly Monitoring Facilities

**Functional Requirement**  
**Role:** System  
**Requirement ID:** FR-04  
**Title:** Provide Carer Interface for Monitoring  
**Description:** The system must provide a carer interface that enables family members and carers to check on user activity and receive customizable reports summarizing user behavior for remote monitoring.  
**Citation:** Curumsing, M. K., et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. [Section 4.1]

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
**Role:** System  
**Requirement ID:** NFR-01  
**Title:** Privacy and Data Security  
**Description:** The system must ensure the privacy of user data by securing information collected through sensors, restricting access to authorized personnel only, and preventing unauthorized access or hacking of sensitive data.  
**Citation:** Curumsing, M. K., et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. [Section 4.2, 5.2.5]  
**Related Title:** Secure IoT Health Data System for Hospital Environments

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
**Role:** System  
**Requirement ID:** NFR-02  
**Title:** System Integration into Daily Life  
**Description:** The system must be well-integrated into the daily lives of elderly users, minimizing complexity and frustration by ensuring that interactions (e.g., messages) are non-intrusive and part of their routine.  
**Citation:** Curumsing, M. K., et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. [Section 5.2.3]  
**Related Title:** User-Friendly Smart Home Interface for Assisted Living Communities

**Requirement ID:** NFR-03  
**Title:** Emotional Goal: Feeling Safe

**Citation:** Curumsing, M. K., et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. [Section 5.2.2]
