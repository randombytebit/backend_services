3. **Assess Runtime Reliability:** Runtime reliability involves ensuring that the AI system performs consistently under varying conditions (e.g., power failures as noted on Page 11, Section 5.2.7) and provides confidence in its predictions or actions, particularly for critical tasks like emergency alerts.
4. **Define Testing Methodologies:** The paper mentions trials with sensor data and checklists to validate AI accuracy (Page 8, Section 4.3.1). Additionally, metamorphic testing or similar techniques can be inferred for validating AI outputs where direct ground truth may not be available.
5. **Establish Confidence-Estimation Constraints:** Given the emotional impact (e.g., feeling safe), the AI must provide confidence scores or trustworthiness indicators for its predictions at runtime (inferred from the need for reassurance, Page 10, Section 5.2.2). This aligns with high-reliability ML systems where individual prediction reliability is crucial.
6. **Map to Related Titles:** Requirements must be contextualized to domains where high-reliability ML is critical, such as healthcare monitoring systems or other smart home solutions for vulnerable populations.

**Extracted Requirements**

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role:** System (AI Component of SofiHub)  
- **Requirement ID:** NFR-01  
- **Title:** Systematic Validation of AI Outputs  
- **Description:** The system must support systematic validation of its AI outputs (e.g., anomaly detection, reminders) through structured testing methodologies such as ground-truth comparison using sensor data and daily checklists during real-world trials, and metamorphic testing to ensure correctness of outputs in scenarios without direct ground truth. Validation must confirm that outputs align with emotional goals like safety and reassurance.  
- **Citation:** Curumsing et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. (Page 8, Section 4.3.1; Page 10, Section 5.2.2)  
- **Related Title:** Healthcare Monitoring Systems, Smart Home Solutions for Elderly Care  

**Non-Functional Requirement**  
- **Role:** System (AI Component of SofiHub)  
- **Requirement ID:** NFR-02  
- **Title:** Runtime Reliability and Confidence Estimation  
- **Description:** The system must ensure runtime reliability by estimating the confidence and trustworthiness of individual AI predictions (e.g., anomaly detection for falls or unusual behavior) at runtime, providing indicators or alerts to users or carers when confidence falls below a predefined threshold (e.g., 90% confidence level for critical alerts). This ensures emotional goals such as feeling safe and reassured are met consistently, even under conditions like power disruptions.  
- **Citation:** Curumsing et al. (2019). Emotion-oriented requirements engineering: A case study in developing a smart home system for the elderly. The Journal of Systems & Software, 147, 215–229. (Page 10, Section 5.2.2; Page 11, Section 5.2.7)  
- **Related Title:** Emergency Response AI Systems, High-Reliability IoT Platforms for Aged Care
